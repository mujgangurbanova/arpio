How Arpio Works

Cross-Region & Cross-Account Recovery

Arpio curates an exact replica of your entire AWS environment – including data & infrastructure – enabling rapid failover that eliminates downtime.

5 Steps to Using Arpio

1

Connect Arpio to your AWS environment

Arpio orchestrates cross-region and cross-account recovery within your AWS environment. To get started, we’ll generate a CloudFormation template that you’ll install to grant us minimal access.

Is this secure?
Yes. We can’t access your data, share your data externally, or do destructive things in your production environment. Our access allows us to survey your infrastructure, instruct AWS to copy your data to your recovery environment, and re-create everything as necessary. Your environment is safe because we’ve engineered Arpio to not need unsafe access.

2

Select resources to protect

Once connected, Arpio presents a list of resources within your production environment. Your last task is to select resources that are important for your workload.

You’ll only need to specify a couple of top-level resources. Arpio understands AWS dependencies, and we’ll find the rest for you. For example, point us at a load balancer and we’ll find the servers, subnets, SSL cert, security groups, and everything else needed to protect that entire workload.

Which AWS resource types does Arpio support?
Arpio works today with resources from EC2, EBS, RDS, ECS, ECR, ELB, VPC, IAM, ACM, Autoscaling, Cognito, ElastiCache, Elastic Beanstalk, and CloudWatch. We’re launching EFS and EKS next. If we don’t yet support your workload, let us know and we can make it a priority.

3

Arpio starts to replicate your environment

At this point, Arpio takes over and captures your first “recovery point.” A recovery point is a snapshot of your entire environment (infrastructure & data). Arpio copies the recovery point directly to your recovery environment. Data never leaves your accounts.

Once the recovery point is copied, Arpio will create the no-cost AWS resources immediately. Resources that cost money won’t get created until you need them.

Where is my data stored?
Your data is stored in your recovery account in the recovery region. For most services, we use the native cold-storage provided by the service. For example, EBS data is stored as EBS snapshots where it can be quickly recovered when you need it.

4

Run a test

Once your first recovery point is created, it’s time to run a test. Simply click “test” in the Arpio UI to bring up your entire recovery environment as quickly as AWS can launch your servers. When you’ve validated that’s it working, another button quickly tears it down so you won’t have to pay for it.

Will my application work?
Most applications work on first launch, but sometimes a little troubleshooting may be required. Arpio does a pretty great job of creating an identical environment, reconnecting all infrastructure and preserving endpoints like IP addresses. Arpio also creates DNS aliases for RDS databases and ElastiCache nodes so your database connection details are still valid in your recovery environment.

Will this impact my production workload?
No. Your real users will continue hitting your production environment at this time. Arpio is just spinning up a replica for you to test.

5

Failover when needed

When you need it, Arpio is standing by to restore your service. One click in the Arpio UI will bring up your entire recovery environment in minutes.

If my region is down, will Arpio still work?
Yes. Arpio runs redundantly across 3 AWS regions, and we don’t protect your production environment from your production region.

Does Arpio update my DNS records?
The last step of any failover is to update DNS to point at the recovered environment. We leave that step to you so that you can validate it’s working before moving traffic over. But we expose every new load balancer endpoint, elastic IP, and public server address in our UI so those details are convenient.
